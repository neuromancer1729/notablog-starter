Firefox OS Developer Preview

Posted on Mon, Mar 17, 2014 review

Few weeks ago, I applied for the following program by firefox wherein, they were (I believe they still are) giving avay firefox OS powered phones for porting existing phonegap/cordova apps to their platform.

I got mine few days ago, and I have to admit firefox OS looks very promising. I hardly lags, games made in html5 runs smoothly, even better than they do in browser. The future looks great for this mobile OS, the force is strong with this one.

Here are few pictures of it.

Its Keon by geeksphone, and the design is really thoughtful too. You can learn more about the phone and its specs on its¬†wikipedia page.

The currest mobile ecosystem is mainly dominated by Apple and Google, and both of them are trying very hard to create walled gardens for their benefits. Android 4.4+ are based on Chrome/blink for the WebView but they deactivated the hardware acceleration for Canvas2D and removed WebGL support, as well as Web Audio API.¬†link

iOS 4+ support WebGL in the webkit engine, but it's only enabled for iAds. And the reason you ask ? because there's no easy way for Apple to monetize a WebGL game.

It really is sad.Internet Explorer supports WebGL on ARM-based Windows tablets manufactured by Microsoft itself, yet the iPad still lacks WebGL

And as if that wasn't enoough, lets talk about what happened to android. Since android is pseudo-opensource, the manufacturers try to fuck up the phone UI is every way possible, and each one of them does it in a more creative way than the previous.(see Samsung's¬†TouchWiz UI). With no-one powerful snough to enforce standards, they have all right to do so.

Apple is good in this regard, atleast they know their design stuff.

The conclusion is that users have little choice when choosing their mobile OS, you have to choose between manufacturer, and weigh out a lot of different stuff. Its not like you bought a laptop and you can posssibly run any OS in it.

Firefox OS is a move to make exactly that possible.Since it is heavily based on popular web technologies and its realatively easier to make and modify it than say AOSP, there shall be more developers interacting with it. And history is proof that only two models of projects are usable, the ones that are purely monopilistic (iOS) and the ones which are hugely audience driven (like what firefox OS would be.) Android is somewhere in the middle, although with google backing it, it will stay on and only get better with time.

Needless to say, exciting adventures lie ahead.

And I am betting on firefox.
