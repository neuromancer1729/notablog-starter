Product development thesis

Before launch

So you have an idea that you are excited about and want to turn it into a product. Well there are many ways to do it, but few ways to do it right. Here;s what i've learned what works best. There are 3 main steps involved.

Start by expanding your ideas on piece of paper, just freehand it out.

This will help you get a early draft of how the end user shall use the product.

Next step is to take this early draft to the designer and develop wireframes.

The meetings with the designer should be all about the end user functionality and covering all corners of the product features, identifying which features are crucial for the MVP and which ones to get rid of.

Develop wireframes as if you're building a monument and not an apartment complex.

User Interviews

Having conversations with early users will help you identify which features to get rid of and which ones to prioritize.

PRD

Now that you have the wireframes, next you need to come up with a Product Requirement Document. For instance here is a PRD of an interview scheduling app

While working on the PRD, you will lay out exactly how the user is supposed to use your product (think out of the box here) in User stories. Do market research while identifying competitors and studying their features, and list down KPIs , goals and metrics.

Timeline Estimation

Know that most developers are bad at estimating time required to complete a task due to biases towards new and shiny technologies. Depending on your technical awareness you can have better conversations with the developers where you point out improvements in the estimation process.

After launch

Kaizen is a Japanese business philosophy of continuous improvement of working practices, personal efficiency, etc.

Measure KPIs, interview live users (plug YC company) and constantly improve the product.
